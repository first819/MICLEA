The Problem.

Practicing alone is a slow, unreliable way to get ready for interviews.

90s is how long it takes most interviewers to form a first impression — long before you reach the answer you actually rehearsed.
01
No reps that count

You rehearse in your head, then burn your few real interviews learning what nerves do to you. There's nowhere safe to practice when it actually matters.

02
Blind to your own habits

The filler words, the rushed pace, the answer that trailed off — you never hear them yourself. The mistakes that cost you the offer are the ones you can't see.

03
Silence after a "no"

Rejections rarely come with reasons. You're left guessing what went wrong, so you walk into the next one repeating the exact same mistakes.
